Tunnel Ship is a short little arcade game that can be played on your computer browser!

In Tunnel Ship you play as a rock mining ship sent in to clear out some debris, only to find themself having to face an asteroid belt from both ends. How many rocks can you destroy and how long can you survive!

Tunnel Ship has two control schemes, one where you can use just the keyboard to play and another where you can use an arduino controller to play!

The game was designed with both of these control schemes in mind.

The player is able to fire a laser that once it collides with rocks it blows them up, rewarding the player with 100 points. The player is able to flip their facing direction which will be needed as the space rocks are coming towards the player in both directions.

The game progressively gets harder for every 10 rocks you destroy. Rocks get more numerous and faster, to compensate the player’s laser fires faster.

– Controls –

Z - Fire

X - Flip

← - Move Left

→ - Move Right

↑ - Move Up

↓ - Move Down

Future Plans

This project was actually going to have a good chunk more added to it. If I was given the time to expand it, here is a list of a few things I would add to the game.

Arduino Analog Control - For starters I would add analog control to the arduino hardware portion of the project; This actually was going to be a feature for the MVP but due to extreme input delay and time constraints it was scrapped and left as just the buttons. This means the only way to move is by using the keyboard arrow keys.

More obstacles/Enemies - For the future of the project I would’ve added another obstacle for the player to avoid/shoot, This would be enemy ships. These ships would only move left and right and would spawn in waves once the previous wave was destroyed. These ships would’ve also had fired lasers back at the player. These laser would have the unique interaction of destroying enemy ships and rocks on the other side of the screen.
